# Continuous Discovery Habits

- **Author:** Teresa Torres
- **Edition:** 2021
- **Field:** Product management — discovery practice as a weekly habit

## Thesis
Discovery is not a quarterly research project — it is a weekly habit
built on three practices: continuous customer touchpoints, an
opportunity solution tree that connects outcomes to bets, and
assumption testing instead of solution testing.

## Core ideas
- **Weekly customer touchpoints are non-negotiable.** A team that
  doesn't talk to customers every week isn't doing discovery. Three
  interviews per week is a workable cadence.
- **Outcomes, not output, drive everything.** Define a clear,
  measurable, time-bounded outcome before generating opportunities.
- **Opportunity Solution Tree (OST).** The book's signature artifact.
  At the top: outcome. Below: opportunities (customer needs / pains /
  desires discovered in interviews). Below those: candidate solutions.
  Below those: assumption tests for each solution. The tree makes
  thinking visible and prevents jumping straight to features.
- **Story-based interviewing.** Ask about past behavior in concrete
  past stories — not future intent ("would you use this?"), which is
  unreliable. "Tell me about the last time you…" is the prompt that
  works.
- **Assumption mapping.** For every candidate solution, list desirability,
  viability, feasibility, usability, ethical assumptions; rank by risk;
  test the riskiest first with the cheapest test.
- **Test assumptions, not solutions.** Most "MVPs" test the whole
  solution and learn ambiguously. A focused assumption test gives a
  clean yes/no.
- **Triangulation.** Combine qual interviews, quant data, and customer
  artifacts before deciding. Single-source decisions are fragile.
- **Make thinking visible.** The tree, the assumption map, the interview
  notes — externalize them so the team thinks together.

## Named frameworks & heuristics
- **Opportunity Solution Tree (OST)** — the central contribution.
- **Story-based interviewing.**
- **Assumption mapping** (rank by risk before testing).
- **Continuous discovery cadence** (weekly touchpoints).

## How to apply
- Schedule three customer interviews this week and every week. Recruit
  from existing customers if research budget is a blocker.
- For your current outcome, draft an OST: outcome → 3–5 opportunities
  → 1–2 candidate solutions per opportunity → assumption tests.
- For every interview, ask only about past concrete experiences. Never
  pitch the product.
- Before any solution build, write the assumption you're testing and
  what evidence falsifies it.

## When to reach for it
Any product team running discovery; especially teams that found
*Lean Startup* vague or struggled to operationalize *Inspired*. The
book is the most operational of the three.

## Caveats
- Dense in process. Pace yourself; it rewards application between
  chapters more than a full read-through.
- Best paired with practice — concepts are obvious in isolation, hard
  in habit.
- Some examples are B2C-flavored; translate to B2B / operator software
  yourself.
