# Problem-first — First Artifact

You're at the start of Path A. Before any pixels, the job is to confirm the problem is real and the audience is specific. This artifact gets you to a defensible **target customer + one-sentence value proposition**, plus a starter **Opportunity Solution Tree** and the **interview prompts** that will populate it.

## Source

- *Inspired* (Cagan) — outcomes-over-output mindset.
- *Continuous Discovery Habits* (Torres) — the tree, story-based interviewing.
- *The Lean Product Playbook* (Olsen) — target customer + value-prop sentence.

---

## Step 1 — Outcome statement

```
We want [WHO] to change their behavior so they [WHAT THEY DO INSTEAD],
measured by [METRIC].
```

If you can't define the metric, the outcome is too vague to act on. Sharpen it.

**Examples:**
- "We want first-time field techs to reach a confident root-cause within 15 minutes on-site, measured by avg-time-to-RCA in our pipeline logs."
- "We want small-business owners to file expenses weekly instead of quarterly, measured by weeks/quarter with ≥1 expense logged."

---

## Step 2 — Target customer

Be specific to the point of discomfort. NOT "small business owners." YES "10-person dental practices in the US managing insurance claims."

```
Target customer:   [SPECIFIC ARCHETYPE — role, size, situation, context]
Underserved need:  [WHAT THEY CURRENTLY DO BADLY OR PAINFULLY]
Importance:        [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — how much do they actually care?]
Current solutions: [HOW THEY HANDLE IT TODAY — spreadsheets? not at all? a competitor?]
```

If "current solutions" is empty or "they don't" — that's either a real green field, or a sign the problem doesn't matter enough. Find out which.

---

## Step 3 — Value proposition (one sentence — the hardest step)

```
For [target customer] who [underserved need], our product is a
[category] that [single key benefit] unlike [primary alternative].
```

Rules:
- One sentence.
- No more than ONE "and."
- The customer should agree with it, not just nod politely.

---

## Step 4 — Opportunity Solution Tree (starter)

```
                  [OUTCOME from Step 1]
                          |
       +-----------+------+------+-----------+
       |                  |                  |
 [OPPORTUNITY 1]   [OPPORTUNITY 2]   [OPPORTUNITY 3]
   (a real           (a real           (a real
    customer pain    customer pain    customer pain
    you have HEARD)  you have HEARD)  you have HEARD)
       |                  |                  |
   [solutions —      [solutions —      [solutions —
    later, not yet]   later, not yet]   later, not yet]
```

**Rule:** opportunities are pains you've HEARD a real customer describe, not inferred from a market report or your own intuition. If you haven't done interviews yet, fill in placeholders and treat Step 5 as your urgent next move.

---

## Step 5 — Interview prompts (story-based)

Schedule **three** interviews this week. Recruit from existing customers, your network, or cold outreach to the archetype in Step 2.

Ask, in order:

1. "Tell me about the last time you [DOMAIN ACTIVITY]. Walk me through what happened, start to finish."
2. "What was the hardest part of that?"
3. "What did you try? What worked? What didn't?"
4. "How did you decide what to do next when you were stuck?"
5. "If a magic wand could fix one thing about that experience, what would it be?"

**Hard rule:** never ask "would you use a product that does X?" — future-intent questions are unreliable. Past behavior only.

---

## What "done" looks like

- [ ] Outcome statement with a real metric.
- [ ] Target customer specific enough that you could name an actual one by tomorrow.
- [ ] Value-prop sentence you'd defend at a whiteboard.
- [ ] Three opportunities sourced from real interviews (not guesses).
- [ ] Three interviews scheduled or completed.

## Next

Once you have a defensible value prop and ≥3 interviews completed, you have earned permission to start designing. Re-run `/design-path` — your constraint will likely shift to **Human-first** (now design for cognition) or **Craft-first** (now ship something to validate the value prop).
